# Threads storytelling system style

Use this skill when Basim asks to write Threads drafts inspired by high-performing storytelling accounts such as @nafkahnation.dx, especially for AI tools, automation, Meta Ads follow-up, owner fatigue, digital systems, OpenClaw, Hermes, and business process problems.

## Core principle

Do not start from a generic tip or service pitch. Start from a **human story with stakes**.

Strong formula:

```text
Orang dekat / kasus nyata-ish
+ masalah yang terlihat biasa
+ korban / kerugian
+ twist: "lo kira A, padahal B"
+ bongkar cara kerja sistem
+ pelajaran praktis / soft CTA
```

The goal is not to copy another creator's exact voice, but to adapt the mechanics: curiosity, conflict, hidden system, and concrete consequence.

## Style rules for Basim

- Write in Indonesian, casual and human.
- Prefer "temen gw", "ada owner", "salah satu bisnis", "pernah ada yang cerita" over abstract openings.
- Keep the tone like curhat/observasi, not polished brochure copy.
- Avoid sounding like a hard-selling agency post.
- Show the **victim**: owner, leads, customer, sales, admin, person trying to learn AI, team member who keeps waiting.
- Show the **loss**: time wasted, leads leaking, owner exhausted, learning energy gone, project revised endlessly, money spent without system.
- Add a twist: "masalahnya bukan X, tapi Y".
- Explain the hidden system/mechanism simply.
- Put data only after story if needed; do not open with statistics unless the user asks.
- End with a light question or soft CTA when useful: "Ada yang pernah ngalamin?", "Bagian mana yang paling sering bocor?", "Kalau ini familiar, mungkin mulai dari beresin sistem kecil dulu."

## General-audience Basim positioning

When Basim asks for content that ordinary people, beginner entrepreneurs, or busy multi-business owners can understand, translate technical AI/automation ideas into daily business burdens.

- Target readers: pemula bisnis, owner yang pegang banyak bisnis, admin/sales yang kebanjiran chat, and people who need AI help but do not know technical setup.
- Prefer simple phrases: "alat bantu AI", "kerjaan berulang", "catatan customer", "follow up", "laporan harian", "tim nggak perlu nunggu owner", "sistem kecil".
- Avoid or explain heavy terms such as API, server, terminal, extension, product engineering, MVP, requirements, and scope.
- Frame AI as practical help: drafting customer replies, summarizing chats, rekap order, reminder follow up, laporan harian, idea/caption drafts, and prioritizing owner tasks.
- The content should feel like business observation/curhat, not a tech tutorial or agency brochure.
- For this audience, good openings include: "Ada orang baru mulai usaha", "Ada owner yang punya beberapa bisnis", "Ada usaha yang chat-nya ramai", "Banyak owner sekarang dengar kata AI di mana-mana".

When Basim explicitly prioritizes fast growth over his normal profile/positioning, broaden the subject matter beyond AI/business while keeping the same mechanics. High-growth pillars: daily health habits, money leaks/paylater, algorithms and digital addiction, products designed to be hard to stop, and slow cumulative risks. The account promise becomes: “membongkar cara kerja hal-hal biasa yang ternyata punya sistem/risiko/pola tersembunyi.” For the search-first radar and approval workflow, see `threads-content-and-scheduling/references/basim-viral-radar-growth-workflow.md`.

## Recommended Basim angles

1. **AI tools/install pain**
   - Victim: beginner AI learner, owner, creator, freelancer.
   - Loss: energy habis untuk install/error before learning.
   - Twist: "Belajar AI-nya belum mulai; kalah duluan di setup."

2. **Owner becomes the manual system**
   - Victim: owner and team.
   - Loss: every decision and report returns to owner.
   - Twist: "Bukan timnya nggak bisa; sistemnya belum bantu."

3. **Meta Ads leak after leads arrive**
   - Victim: leads/customer/sales/owner.
   - Loss: budget ads burns, leads not followed up.
   - Twist: "Iklannya tidak selalu gagal; yang bocor sistem setelah leads masuk."

4. **Digital project chaos**
   - Victim: owner, vendor, team.
   - Loss: revision loops and budget/time waste.
   - Twist: "Coding belum tentu masalahnya; ekspektasi awalnya yang belum rapi."

5. **Apps/tools disconnected**
   - Victim: owner/admin.
   - Loss: all tools exist but owner becomes the bridge.
   - Twist: "Bukan butuh tambah aplikasi; butuh nyambungin yang sudah ada."

## Draft structure

For single-post Threads, use 250–500 characters when scheduling through Repliz unless user asks for long thread.

Template:

```text
Temen gw / Ada owner / Pernah ada yang cerita [situasi].

Awalnya dia pikir masalahnya [dugaan umum].

Ternyata bukan itu.

Masalahnya [hidden system / actual bottleneck].

[2–4 concrete examples]

[lesson / question / soft CTA]
```

For multi-post threads:

1. Post 1: story + strong twist.
2. Post 2: explain hidden system.
3. Post 3: concrete examples.
4. Post 4: lesson + soft CTA.

Basim viral radar default (updated from Basim request): when drafting viral radar candidates inspired by @nafkahnation.dx, prefer longer 8–12 part story threads instead of short 3–4 part mini-threads. Build a real narrative arc: human case → surface problem → victim/loss → reversal “dikira A padahal B” → hidden system → concrete examples → cumulative consequence → practical/shareable lesson. Keep each part natural and under Repliz-safe length; do not add visible numbering unless asked.

## Quality checklist

Before presenting final drafts:

- Is there a person/case, not just a topic?
- Is there a victim or clear loss?
- Is there a twist from common assumption to real problem?
- Is the mechanism explained in plain language?
- Does it fit Basim's positioning: AI tools, automation, digital systems, owner fatigue, execution?
- If likely scheduled via Repliz, count characters with a tool and keep each main post under 500 characters.

## Pitfalls

- Do not copy @nafkahnation.dx wording wholesale.
- Do not overuse fearmongering; keep it grounded and relevant.
- Do not invent specific real people, names, medical/financial claims, or statistics. Use generic "temen gw" / "ada owner" unless user provides a true case.
- Do not make claims that require evidence unless you can verify them.
- Avoid repetitive CTA reply in every post unless Basim explicitly asks.
