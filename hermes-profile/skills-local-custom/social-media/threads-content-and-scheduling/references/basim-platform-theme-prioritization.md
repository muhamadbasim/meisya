# Basim platform-specific theme prioritization

Use this reference when Basim asks to brainstorm/prioritize social-media themes, especially phrasing like `tema prioritas`, `cari yang viral`, `sesuai platform`, or when maintaining Repliz cron prompts.

## Core principle

Do not chase viral topics raw. Prioritize themes using:

```text
viral signal + Basim positioning fit + platform-native format + recent account performance
```

For Basim, the durable positioning fit is beginner/business-owner recognition: owner fatigue, manual systems, lead follow-up leaks, practical AI, and content that makes the reader feel `ini gue banget`.

## Required workflow

1. Audit recent performance first when Repliz analytics are available, ideally last 48 hours.
   - Identify top posts by platform and topic cluster.
   - Note weak platform issues separately (e.g. YouTube metadata/title problems) rather than forcing volume.
2. Search/verify current viral/trend context lightly.
   - Use trend context as angle fuel, not as the account direction.
   - If search results are noisy, say so and rely more heavily on internal performance data.
3. Cluster themes at the idea level before drafting.
4. Map the same theme differently per platform; never copy one caption everywhere.
5. Explicitly mark what to avoid, not just what to post.

## Basim priority clusters learned from June 2026 audit

### 1. AI manusiawi

Best when framed as: AI can speed work up, but content still needs human context and lived experience.

Angles:
- Konten AI yang bagus masih kelihatan ada manusia di belakangnya.
- Masalah konten AI sering bukan tool, tapi konteks kosong.
- Clear context beats clever prompts.

Platform fit:
- Threads: short spontaneous observation.
- TikTok: quick hook + example.
- Instagram: saveable checklist/framework.
- YouTube Shorts: problem title like `Kenapa Konten AI Sering Terasa Palsu?`.

### 2. Owner bisnis masih jadi sistem manual

Best for monetizable positioning. Anchor on owner fatigue, not abstract automation.

Angles:
- Kalau semua chat/follow-up harus diingat owner, owner sudah jadi sistemnya.
- Bisnis kecil sering bukan kurang rajin, tapi prosesnya terlalu manual.
- Kalau owner sakit lalu bisnis berhenti, sistemnya belum jalan.

### 3. Lead bocor dari iklan

Strong bridge between business pain and digital/system services.

Angles:
- Iklan mungkin tidak gagal; follow-up yang bocor.
- Customer sudah klik, tapi dibalas terlambat.
- Banyak bisnis bayar lead lalu kehilangan lead di WhatsApp.

### 4. Prompt AI untuk pemula

Keep it practical and jargon-free.

Basim-friendly prompt lesson:
`buat siapa, tujuannya apa, gaya bahasanya gimana, formatnya mau apa`.

### 5. Reactive AI hype, e.g. new models/tools

Use for Threads and light TikTok reactions. Do not over-explain model specs unless the audience asks.

Example pattern:
`AI model baru keluar terus, tapi problem bisnis kecil masih sama: chat customer berantakan / follow-up bocor / konteks konten kosong.`

### 6. Budget/keluarga/calendar topics

Use only as practical side themes when there is real public momentum. Tie to concrete everyday consequences, not random news commentary.

## Platform mapping defaults

### Threads

- 1–3 sentences.
- Spontaneous/curhat tone.
- Works well for reactive AI comments and owner-fatigue observations.
- Avoid polished framework posts unless user asked for thread form.

### TikTok

- Hook in the first second.
- Slideshow/video with one concrete pain per slide/beat.
- Strong themes: AI creator for beginners, owner manual work, lead follow-up leaks, budget/calendar practicalities.

### Instagram

- Carousel must be saveable.
- Prefer checklist, signs, mistakes, templates, or frameworks.
- Weak format: reflective advice with no steps.

### YouTube Shorts

- Use searchable/problem-based titles.
- Never allow `unknown` titles in Repliz metadata.
- Reduce quantity until title/retention issues are fixed; quality and metadata matter more than volume.

## 3-day starter rotation

Day 1: AI manusiawi
- Threads: `AI makin pintar, tapi konten yang paling ngena tetap yang kelihatan pernah dialami manusia.`
- TikTok: `Kalau konten AI kamu terasa kaku, masalahnya mungkin bukan di tools.`
- IG: `5 cara bikin konten AI terasa lebih manusia`
- YouTube: `Kenapa Konten AI Sering Terasa Palsu?`

Day 2: Owner manual
- Threads: `Kadang owner bukan capek jualannya. Capeknya karena semua proses kecil masih harus dia ingat sendiri.`
- TikTok: `Kalau bisnis berhenti saat owner nggak buka WhatsApp, berarti sistemnya belum jalan.`
- IG: `Tanda bisnismu butuh sistem, bukan tambah admin`
- YouTube: `Tanda Bisnismu Terlalu Bergantung ke Owner`

Day 3: Lead follow-up leak
- Threads: `Banyak bisnis bayar mahal buat dapat lead, lalu kehilangan lead itu karena telat follow-up.`
- TikTok: `Iklan kamu mungkin nggak gagal. Bisa jadi follow-up-nya yang bocor.`
- IG: `5 titik bocor setelah customer klik iklan`
- YouTube: `Kenapa Lead dari Iklan Sering Hilang?`

## Pitfalls

- Do not make Basim's account a generic news account.
- Do not let viral topics override positioning.
- Do not reuse one caption across Threads, TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube.
- Do not present noisy search results as strong evidence; label them as weak/noisy and lean on Repliz analytics.
- Do not post/schedule manual drafts without Basim's explicit approval gate from the main skill.
