# Emotional Threads Patterns

Use when drafting Indonesian Threads posts intended to feel close, reflective, and emotionally resonant without becoming melodramatic or manipulative.

## Core principle

Aim for reader recognition before persuasion: the reader should think "ini gue banget" before they see any service angle.

## Emotional levers that worked

- **Delayed idea:** an idea that once felt exciting but slowly weakened because it stayed in the person's head too long.
- **Silent fatigue:** business owners look fine outside, but repeatedly handle small manual burdens: lost chats, repeated questions, scattered data, owner bottlenecks.
- **Guilt without blame:** people feel guilty for not starting, but the framing should reassure them they may not be lazy; the idea may simply need to be broken into smaller steps.
- **Fear of exposure:** the person is afraid the idea will sound stupid, too ambitious, immature, or not realistic once explained aloud.
- **Regret trigger:** years later they see someone else build something similar and think, "Dulu aku juga pernah kepikiran ini."
- **Disappointment in misalignment:** everyone says they understand the project, but founder, team, user, and builder imagine different things; the emotional cost is broken trust, not just revisions.

## Structure

1. Start with a quiet emotional observation.
2. Name the hidden tension in plain language.
3. List concrete micro-situations, one per line.
4. Reframe the reader's problem gently; avoid blaming them.
5. Offer a small first step: ngobrol, merapikan ide, memecah langkah, finding the most important part.

## Tone guardrails

- Do not overuse dramatic words like "hancur", "trauma", "gagal total" unless the user asks for high drama.
- Avoid brochure phrasing and unsupported claims.
- Keep CTA soft: "mungkin kita bisa mulai dari ngobrol dulu" or "mulai cukup dengan membuatnya lebih jelas."
- Prefer short paragraphs and white space for Threads readability.

## Example hook forms

```text
Ada ide yang dulu bikin kamu semangat.
```

```text
Kadang bagian paling melelahkan dari punya ide bukan membuatnya.
Tapi menyimpannya sendirian terlalu lama.
```

```text
Hal paling sedih dari ide yang tidak pernah dimulai adalah:
kamu tidak pernah benar-benar tahu apakah ide itu bisa berhasil.
```

```text
Ada bisnis yang tumbuh, tapi sistemnya tidak ikut tumbuh.
```
